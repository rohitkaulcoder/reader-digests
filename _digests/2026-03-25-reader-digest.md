### Gulf War: Airfares Soar
**Author:** Zinal Dedhia / The Core
**Reading Time:** ~8 min

#### Key Insights

1. India faces a compounding airspace crisis -- both Middle Eastern and Pakistani airspace are simultaneously closed. The double closure forces reroutes adding ~20% to operating costs from longer flight times, plus another ~10% from landing and crew expenses. If Brent crude crosses $100-120/barrel, aviation fuel surcharges compound these structural costs further.

2. The fare spike is concentrated in a short forward booking window, revealing how thin airline inventory buffers actually are. Delhi-London went from Rs 35,000-45,000 to Rs 85,000-1.2 lakh; Bengaluru-Frankfurt from Rs 40,000-50,000 to Rs 1.5-1.9 lakh for mid-March departures.

3. India's LPG supply chain is simultaneously threatened -- the crisis is expected to halve India's LPG imports in March. Reliance executed India's first purchase of Iranian crude since 2019 (5M barrels under a 30-day US sanctions waiver).

4. India's private sector PMI simultaneously hit a three-year low (56.5, down from 58.9), creating a stagflationary environment with input cost inflation at its fastest pace since mid-2022.

#### Notable Quotes

> "The closure of Middle Eastern airspace has affected Indian aviation significantly, coming as it does on top of the existing shutdown of Pakistani airspace. The Middle East corridor is India's largest westbound route." -- Sanjay Lazar

> "These diversions have already added around 20% to operating costs, with landing and crew expenses adding another 10%." -- Sanjay Lazar

#### One-Line Summary
The West Asia conflict has closed Middle Eastern and Pakistani airspace, triggering a 40%+ spike in European airfares from India and pushing travelers toward Southeast Asian alternatives.

### Another hidden inflation driver
**Author:** Tracy Alloway & Joe Weisenthal, Bloomberg Odd Lots
**Reading Time:** ~6 min

#### Key Insights

1. The Iran War has disrupted naphtha supply -- the feedstock for polyethylene (virtually all consumer plastic packaging). Asian refiners have curtailed output. This is a second-order energy price shock hitting every physical good on a grocery shelf.

2. Packaging inflation is regressive -- a $2 bag of carrots has a higher proportion of its retail price tied to plastic than a $200 pharmaceutical. This hits the bottom of the consumer price spectrum hardest.

3. There is no meaningful demand destruction mechanism for packaging -- modern retail logistics and food safety regulations are built around it, making rising polyethylene costs a near-pure price passthrough.

#### Notable Quotes

> "There's something kind of remarkable (or perhaps dystopian) that in today's economy, even when you're buying carrots, you're really buying crude oil."

#### One-Line Summary
Plastic packaging -- a petroleum derivative -- is an underappreciated inflation transmission mechanism for low-value consumer goods, now squeezed by naphtha shortages.
